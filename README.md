# Adventure-Works-Sales-Analysis-using-Excel.

Designed and built a fully interactive, two-page sales analytics dashboard in Microsoft Excel — without using Power BI — to transform thousands of raw Adventure Works sales records into a decision-ready reporting tool.

Problem: The raw dataset was spread across multiple tables (products, customers, sales, dates) and was too unstructured for quick business decisions. Leadership needed fast answers to questions such as: Who are the most profitable customers? Which products drive the most profit? How is revenue trending month over month? Manually answering these required hours of filtering and re-pivoting each time a question changed.

Approach:
• Power Query – imported, cleaned, and merged multiple raw tables into a single structured dataset
• Power Pivot (Data Model) – built relational connections across tables using a star-schema design
• DAX – created custom measures for Revenue, Profit, Cost, and Profit Share %
• PivotTables & PivotCharts – powered dynamic bar, column, donut, and trend visualizations
• Slicers – added one-click filters for Year, Month, Country, Gender, and Weekday/Weekend
• Applied custom shapes, icons, and number formatting for a clean, modern BI-style layout

Result: A fully interactive dashboard that lets any user — regardless of Excel expertise — filter by year, country, or product and instantly view updated revenue, profit, and customer insights. Reduced what previously took hours of manual reporting to a matter of seconds, while eliminating the risk of formula errors.

This project demonstrates how Excel, when combined with Power Query, Power Pivot, and DAX, can deliver enterprise-level business intelligence capabilities without additional BI software.
